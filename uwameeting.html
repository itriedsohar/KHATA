Uganda Wildlife Authority Reaffirms Partnership with Karamoja Hoteliers and Tourism Association (KHATA)

Kampala, Uganda

25 March,2025

The Uganda Wildlife Authority (UWA) has reiterated its commitment to its strategic partnership with the Karamoja Hoteliers and Tourism Association (KHATA), reinforcing efforts to boost tourism and sustainable wildlife conservation in the Karamoja region.

In a recent statement, UWA Executive Director Dr. James Musinguzi emphasized the importance of collaboration with private sector players like KHATA in promoting eco-tourism and enhancing visitor experiences in Uganda’s national parks.

“Our partnership with KHATA is vital in developing Karamoja’s tourism potential while ensuring that conservation and community benefits go hand in hand By working closely with local hoteliers and tourism stakeholders, we can create a thriving, sustainable tourism economy in the region.”

said Mwandha.



Strengthening Tourism and Conservation

KHATA, an association of hoteliers, lodge owners, and tourism operators in the Karamoja sub-region, has been instrumental in improving hospitality standards and promoting the region’s unique attractions, including Kidepo Valley National Park, Mount Moroto, and the Pian Upe Wildlife Reserve.

Through its collaboration with UWA, KHATA has facilitated tourism marketing, infrastructure development, and community engagement programs, ensuring that visitors receive high-quality services while supporting conservation efforts.

Future Plans for Growth

UWA has pledged to deepen its partnership with KHATA by:
  • Enhancing marketing campaigns to attract more domestic and international tourists to Karamoja.
  • Improving accessibility and tourism infrastructure in and around protected areas.
  • Supporting community-based tourism initiatives to ensure locals benefit from wildlife conservation.


“Karamoja is one of Uganda’s most unique and untapped tourism destinations,”

said a KHATA representative.

“With UWA’s support, we are confident that the region will become a top choice for travelers seeking adventure and cultural experiences.”



A Model for Public-Private Collaboration

The UWA-KHATA partnership serves as a model for how government agencies and private sector players can work together to promote sustainable tourism while preserving Uganda’s natural heritage.

As Uganda continues to position itself as a premier safari and cultural tourism destination, such collaborations are expected to drive economic growth and conservation success in the Karamoja region and beyond.
