KHATA Holds Inaugural All-Hands General Assembly to Chart Karamoja's Tourism Future

Moroto, Uganda

1 March,2025

The Karamoja Hoteliers and Tourism Association (KHATA) convened its first-ever all-hands general assembly today, bringing together stakeholders from across Uganda's northeastern region to develop a unified vision for sustainable tourism growth.

"This historic gathering marks our transition from individual operators to a powerful collective voice," declared KHATA Chairperson, Hon. Lokeris Samson, in opening remarks. "Together, we're building Karamoja's tourism ecosystem from the ground up."



Key Agenda Items
  • Standardization Framework – Drafting region-wide hospitality quality standards


  • Investment Blueprint – Finalizing incentives for hotel development


  • Skills Development – Launching the Karamoja Hospitality Training Initiative


  • Security Collaboration – New tourism police partnership proposal


  • Marketing Strategy – "Visit Karamoja" campaign development



Challenges Addressed

Members openly discussed pressing issues:
  • Electricity reliability for remote lodges


  • Road access improvements


  • Poaching impacts on safari tourism


  • Staff retention strategies


"These frank conversations are exactly why we needed this assembly," noted [Hotel Owner Name] from [Property Name]. "We're solving problems collectively that none of us could tackle alone."



Youth Engagement Focus

A special session highlighted opportunities for young professionals, including:

  • Apprenticeship programs at premier lodges


  • University partnerships for tourism degrees


  • Young Entrepreneurs Fund for tourism startups


Closing Commitments

The assembly concluded with three actionable goals:
  • 25% increase in hotel capacity by 2025


  • Certified training for 500 hospitality workers


  • Unified marketing campaign launch by Q1 2024


"Today we've planted seeds that will grow Karamoja's tourism economy for generations,The real work begins tomorrow."

Summarises Hon. Lokeris Peter, Chairman of KHATA.
