About us

About us

Karimojong Hoteliers and Tours Association (KHATA) is a coalition of indigenous hotel and tour company owners in the Karamoja region, Uganda. Established in 2019 and incorporated in 2023, KHATA is dedicated to enhancing the hospitality and tourism sector in Karamoja. Our members are also affiliated with national bodies such as the Uganda Hotel Owners Association (UHOA), the Association of Uganda Tour Operators (AUTO), the Uganda Tourism Board (UTB), and the Uganda Wildlife Authority (UWA).


Vision

"To empower indigenous hospitality and tourism enterprises in Karamoja by standardizing services, expanding infrastructure, and promoting the region’s unique cultural and natural attractions. Through collaboration, training, and sustainable practices, we aim to create economic opportunities, preserve our heritage, and provide exceptional experiences for visitors from around the world."


Mission

"To establish Karamoja as a premier tourism and hospitality destination in Uganda, where indigenous businesses thrive, cultural heritage is celebrated, and sustainable development uplifts local communities."

Industry overview


KHATA operates within the hospitality, tourism, and conservancy sectors, focusing on:


  • Hospitality – Upgrading and standardizing hotels, lodges, and accommodation services to meet international standards.

  • Tourism – Promoting Karamoja’s cultural, outdoor, and wildlife experiences.

  • Conservancy – Supporting the sustainable management of wildlife and natural heritage sites.

Objectives


  • Standardize and expand – hospitality, tourism, and conservancy services to enhance service delivery.

  • Develop a sustainable funding mechanism – through KHATA’s Revolving Development Fund (KHATA RDF), financed by membership fees, savings, grants, affordable loans, and government tax incentives.

  • Empower local youth and communities –through training in hospitality, tour guiding, culinary arts, and cultural performances to create employment opportunities.

  • Promote Karamoja’s unique attractions, including its cultural heritage, scenic landscapes, wildlife, and mining sites.
