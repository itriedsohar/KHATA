KHATA Engages Key Stakeholders to Boost Tourism and Hotel Development in Karamoja

Karamoja, Uganda

12 April,2025



The Karamoja Hoteliers and Tourism Association (KHATA) is set to hold a strategic meeting with General Salim Saleh, a prominent figure in Uganda's development sector, to discuss tourism growth and hotel infrastructure expansion in the Karamoja sub-region.

The engagement highlights KHATA's commitment to positioning Karamoja as a premier eco-tourism and cultural destination, while addressing critical challenges such as accommodation shortages and investment opportunities for visitors and investors.

General Salim Saleh Endorses KHATA’s Vision

In a pre-meeting statement, General Salim Saleh praised KHATA’s efforts in driving investment and hospitality growth in the region.

"KHATA is big and deserves all the attention of my office," declared General Saleh. "Karamoja’s tourism potential is unmatched—from its wildlife to its heritage. But for visitors to come, we need better hotels, improved infrastructure, and stronger partnerships to unlock this region’s full potential."



Hotel Development: A Priority for Karamoja’s Tourism Growth

KHATA’s delegation will present a five-point plan focusing on:

  • Investment Incentives – Encouraging private sector participation in mid-range and luxury hotel developments across Moroto, Kidepo, and Kotido.



  • Skills Development – Training programs for local staff in hotel management, culinary arts, and hospitality services to elevate service standards.



  • Infrastructure Upgrades – Improving roads, electricity, and water supply to support new hotel projects.


  • Sustainable Tourism – Promoting eco-friendly lodges and community-based tourism initiatives.


  • Marketing Karamoja – A joint campaign with the Uganda Tourism Board to attract domestic and international tourists.


"Investors are increasingly interested in Karamoja, but the lack of quality accommodations remains a major hurdle," said [KHATA Spokesperson]. "With the right partnerships and policies, we can fast-track the development of new hotels and resorts, creating jobs and boosting the local economy."



Next Steps

The discussions are expected to result in a collaborative framework between KHATA, development partners, and the private sector to streamline investments and ensure sustainable growth in the hospitality industry.

"Karamoja is ready for transformation," declared [KHATA Chairperson]. "We call upon hotel investors and tourism stakeholders to join us in unlocking this region’s vast potential."
