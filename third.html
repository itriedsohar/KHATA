KHATA Celebrates Third Anniversary, Marks Milestones in Karamoja Tourism Growth

Moroto, Uganda

30 October,2024

The Karamoja Hoteliers and Tourism Association (KHATA) today celebrated its third anniversary, reflecting on its achievements in transforming the region’s tourism sector and strengthening partnerships for sustainable development.

The event, held at [Venue Name], brought together hotel owners, tour operators, government officials, and conservation partners, including the Uganda Wildlife Authority (UWA), to commemorate KHATA’s contributions to promoting Karamoja as a premier travel destination.

A Journey of Growth and Collaboration

Since its inception in [2021], KHATA has played a pivotal role in:


  • Elevating hospitality standards through training and capacity-building for hotels and lodges.


  • Marketing Karamoja’s unique attractions, including Kidepo Valley National Park, Mount Moroto, and cultural experiences.


  • Advocating for improved tourism infrastructure, such as better roads and visitor facilities.


  • Fostering partnerships with UWA, local governments, and development agencies to boost eco-tourism.


In his keynote address, KHATA Chairperson [Name] said:

“Three years ago, we envisioned a united voice for Karamoja’s tourism sector. Today, we celebrate stronger collaboration, increased visitor numbers, and a growing recognition of our region’s potential. This is just the beginning.”



A Call for Continued Support

KHATA urged the government and private sector to increase investment in Karamoja’s tourism infrastructure, emphasizing the need for better air connectivity, upgraded lodges, and stronger security to sustain growth.
